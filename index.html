Deriv Binary Options

Matches vs. Differs - Matches predicts the same digit repeated, Differs predicts a different digit than the last result. Use Matches during strong momentum and Differs when the market is sideways or noisy.

Over vs. Under - Over forecasts digits above threshold 5, Under forecasts digits below threshold 5. Over is useful when momentum pushes prices higher; Under is useful in declining or mean-reverting conditions.

Even vs. Odd - Even targets digits 0,2,4,6,8 while Odd targets 1,3,5,7,9. This strategy is useful when there is symmetry or alternating patterns in the tick sequence.

Risk note: Binary options can return fixed payouts but lose the full stake if the condition is not met. Always size positions and treat these trades as high-risk, high-reward opportunities.

Schrödinger Wave Equation

We conceptually map probability amplitude to each digit as a wave function Ψ. Volatility acts like potential energy, shaping the likelihood of each outcome.

Time-dependent Schrödinger equation:

iℏ (∂Ψ/∂t) = Ĥ Ψ

In our model, waveFrequency controls oscillation behavior and fermiTemperature affects spread and uncertainty. The computeProbabilities method mimics wave collapse by normalizing the probability distribution and selecting the digit with highest amplitude.

Equation mapping:

  • Ψ(digit, t) ↔ probability amplitude for each digit
  • Ĥ ↔ quantum-inspired operator using volatility and frequency
  • iℏ ∂Ψ/∂t ↔ time evolution of the digit probability distribution

Our algorithm uses a combination of Fermi-Dirac weighting, wave interference, and normalization to model digital wave collapse. The final predicted digit is the state with the highest normalized probability.
